File Deletion via Shred

Last updated 2 months ago on 2026-03-24
Created 6 years ago on 2020-04-27

About

Malware or other files dropped or created on a system by an adversary may leave traces behind as to what was done within a network and how. Adversaries may remove these files over the course of an intrusion to keep their footprint low or remove them at the end as part of the post-intrusion cleanup process.

✄𐘗process where host.os.type == "linux" and event.type == "start" and event.action in ("exec", "exec_event", "start", "ProcessRollup2", "executed", "process_started") and
process.name == "shred" and (
// Any short-flag cluster containing at least one of u/z, and containing no extra "-" after the first one
process.args regex~ "-[^-]*[uz][^-]*" or
process.args in ("--remove", "--zero")
) and
not process.parent.name == "logrotate"
